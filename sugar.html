Sugar - for the food industry and beyond

We supply various types of sugar from European and American producers, with different colors and other parameters, but always of the highest quality. White, brown, beetroot and cane.

About

Sugar, which we have directly from sugar cane producers, is a natural source of energy and plays a vital role in the food, pharmaceutical and beverage industries.
South America is one of the largest producers of sugar in the world. They produce food that feeds and sweetens the lives of hundreds of millions of people around the world.

Beet sugar production?

This is a multi-stage process that requires special apparatus. The process begins with harvesting the sugar beet, which is washed and cleaned of light and heavy impurities. The beet is cut into smaller pieces and a so-called cuttings is formed, which are introduced into a diffuser. There, the sugar is leached by using water at an elevated temperature (75-85 °C).

Result

The result is a slice and raw juice containing 15% sugar. The juice undergoes further processes, including purification from non-sugar compounds. Further stages involve crystallisation and centrifugation, which ultimately produces crystallised table sugar.

Properties

Beet sugar, although produced from beets, is white in colour.
It is a free-flowing, crystalline substance, available in grocery shops. Its unique characteristic is its sweet taste.
In addition, beet sugar dissolves well in water, has no odour, and when added to foods and beverages does not affect their flavour or colour. This makes it great not only as a sweetener for coffee and tea, but also for sweetening dishes, snacks and desserts in general.

Types

  • Sugar - this term is used to describe a chemical compound composed of two monosaccharide molecules - glucose and fructose. Other names include disaccharide and sucrose.
  • White sugar - this is a crystalline sugar with a high degree of purity. The best quality is 'white sugar 1' or 'white sugar 2'. Higher numbers indicate a poorer quality product. White sugar is also sometimes referred to as refined sugar.
  • Inverted sugar - a combination of glucose and fructose in two equal parts.
  • Candied sugar (kandyz) - sugar obtained through special crystallisation. It is characterised by large sugar crystals.
  • Raw sugar - this is sugar that has undergone a crystallisation process and is characterised by a lower degree of purity. Such sugar is then converted to white sugar.
ICUMSA is a global organisation bringing together the activities of National Sugar Analysis Committees in over thirty member countries.

The work is carried out under different thematic divisions, each headed by a referee. It is recommended that methods are first pre-approved by ICUMSA. Once all the requirements of the Commission have been met, the methods gain official status. Methods that are clearly useful and have found well-established use, or that are not suitable for collaborative testing, are given Accepted status. The ICUMSA grade is an international unit expressing the purity of the sugar in solution and is directly related to the colour of the sugar. Note that there are different types of ICUMSA units. In the case of Brazilian sugar, the lower the ICUMSA value, the whiter the sugar.
